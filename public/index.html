What are IMD Levels?

      
The Index of Multiple Deprivation is the official measure of relative deprivation for small areas in England.
      

      
Deciles are calculated by ranking small areas in England from most deprived to least deprived and dividing
        them
        into 10 equal groups.

      
Why is it relevant to compare IMD levels to education levels?

      
Children who grow up in more deprived areas, i.e., lower IMD deciles, are less likely to pursue higher
        education (1).
        However, education is one of the main avenues for escaping deprivation, thereby perpetuating a vicious cycle
        that is difficult to break.

      
IMD deciles are used by the government to determine where funding should be allocated. Therefore, this tool
        could be used to help decide which areas are most in need.
